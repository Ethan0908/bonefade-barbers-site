Design requirements:
Build a modern, clean, realistic one-page website that matches the company’s price level and audience.

Do not make it futuristic, glassmorphism, neon, cyber, overly animated, or generic SaaS-style.

Adapt the style based on the business:
- Cheap or budget-friendly brands should feel clear, simple, practical, and easy to use.
- Affordable or mid-range brands should feel polished, friendly, and trustworthy.
- Premium brands should feel refined, spacious, elegant, and use better typography.
- Luxury brands should feel minimal, editorial, calm, and high-end, without being flashy.

The page should include:
1. Hero section with strong headline, short supporting text, and clear CTA
2. Brief intro explaining what the company does
3. Services or products section
4. Why choose us / key benefits
5. Social proof, reviews, testimonials, or trust indicators
6. About section
7. Pricing, menu, packages, or featured offerings if relevant
8. Gallery or visual section if useful
9. FAQ section
10. Contact section with CTA
11. Map, address, and opening hours if the business depends on local visits, such as a restaurant, salon, clinic, shop, gym, or repair service
12. Footer with business name, contact info, links, and social media

Visual direction:
Choose colours, fonts, spacing, imagery style, and layout based on the business type and price level. The design should feel appropriate for the real company, not like a template.

Use strong hierarchy, readable text, good spacing, mobile-first responsive layout, accessible contrast, and clear calls to action.

Output:
Generate the complete one-page website with clean HTML, CSS, and JavaScript if needed. Keep the code simple, production-ready, and easy to edit.
